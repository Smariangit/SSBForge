OVERVIEW

What is the SSB Interview?

The Services Selection Board (SSB) is the selection mechanism used by the Indian Armed Forces — Army, Navy, and Air Force — to identify candidates with Officer Like Qualities (OLQs). It is a five-day residential assessment conducted at one of several SSB centres across India.

Unlike a written exam, SSB tests your personality, not your knowledge. Assessors — a psychologist, Group Testing Officer (GTO), and an Interviewing Officer (IO) — independently observe you over five days and give an independent recommendation. Their collective assessment determines your Conference result.

CORE FRAMEWORK

The 15 Officer Like Qualities

Every task in SSB — written, spoken, physical, or group — is designed to reveal the presence or absence of these 15 OLQs. They are grouped into four clusters.

🧠 Planning & Organising
1
Effective Intelligence

Practical, common-sense thinking applied to real situations. Not just IQ — the ability to adapt knowledge to varied contexts.

2
Reasoning Ability

Logical deduction and sound judgement. Drawing correct conclusions from incomplete information.

3
Organising Ability

Marshalling resources — people, time, material — in an efficient and purposeful way.

💪 Social Effectiveness
4
Social Adaptability

Getting along with a wide range of people, adapting behaviour to social context without losing integrity.

5
Cooperation

Willingness to subordinate personal interests for the team. Supporting others, sharing credit.

6
Sense of Responsibility

Taking ownership of tasks and outcomes. Not passing blame. Following through on commitments.

🔥 Dynamic Qualities
7
Initiative

Acting without being told. Spotting what needs to be done and doing it.

8
Self Confidence

Belief in your own ability to handle situations. Expressed through calm decisiveness, not arrogance.

9
Speed of Decision

Making timely decisions under pressure. Indecision is as costly as a wrong decision.

10
Ability to Influence the Group

Moving others toward a goal through conviction, not authority. Persuasion, not domination.

11
Liveliness

Energy, enthusiasm, and a positive outlook that is contagious. Not loudness — genuine vigour.

🎖️ Leadership & Integrity
12
Stamina

Physical and mental endurance. Maintaining performance through fatigue, stress, and discomfort.

13
Courage

Physical and moral courage. Saying what is true, doing what is right even when it is difficult.

14
Determination

Persistent effort toward a goal despite obstacles. The refusal to give up.

15
Sense of Honour & Integrity

Consistency between words and actions. Truthfulness. Living by a personal code of ethics.

Key insight: You cannot fake OLQs over five days. Assessors are trained to spot inconsistency between your psychology results, GTO performance, and interview persona. The only reliable strategy is genuine self-development.
DAY 1

Stage 1 — Screening

Stage 1 is a same-day filter. About 40–60% of candidates are screened in; the rest are sent back immediately. It consists of two tests.

Officer Intelligence Rating (OIR)
Verbal + Non-Verbal~17 minutes totalIndividual

Two booklets — one verbal (analogies, series completion, odd-one-out, coding-decoding) and one non-verbal (figure series, pattern completion, spatial reasoning). Speed and accuracy both matter. The OIR gives you a rating from 1 (best) to 5 (below standard). Aim for OIR Grade 1 or 2.

Practice: Use the OIR module on this site. Aim to complete each booklet in under 8 minutes.
PPDT — Picture Perception & Discussion Test
30s observation4 min writingGroup discussion

A hazy image is shown for 30 seconds. You then write a story (4 minutes) identifying: number of characters, approximate age, mood (positive/negative), and a complete narrative with beginning, middle, and end. Characters must include a hero with strong OLQs.

After writing, your group of 15–20 candidates discusses the image for 15 minutes. Assessors watch how you interact: do you listen, do you push your story forward constructively, do you lead, do you collaborate?

Practice: Write stories with a single decisive protagonist. In GD, enter early, speak clearly, and help build consensus — don't just push your own narrative.
DAY 2 — STAGE 2

Psychological Tests

The psychology battery is conducted on Day 2 morning. Four tests reveal your subconscious thought patterns. Write freely — assessors look for consistency and positivity, not literary quality.

TAT — Thematic Apperception Test
12 images + 1 blank30 seconds per image

A series of ambiguous images is shown. You write one story per image in 30 seconds. The final slide is blank — you write any story you like. Each story must have: a definite hero (who has OLQs), a clear situation, and a positive resolution. Avoid tragedies, passive heroes, or unhelpful characters.

Practice: Your protagonist must act. They must face a challenge, take initiative, and resolve it positively. Never write a story where the hero gives up, dies, or fails morally.
WAT — Word Association Test
60 words15 seconds per word

60 words are shown one by one on a screen, 15 seconds each. Write the first complete sentence that comes to your mind. There is no right or wrong answer — but patterns matter. Positive, action-oriented, socially connected responses reflect healthy OLQs. Avoid negative, aggressive, or self-pitying sentences.

Train your instincts: for every word, practise writing an action-oriented, positive sentence in under 5 seconds. The 15-second pressure is real.
SRT — Situation Reaction Test
60 situations30 minutes total

60 everyday situations are given (e.g., "You see a man drowning in a river nearby"). You write what you would do in a few words. Speed is critical — roughly 30 seconds per situation. Responses must be practical, moral, and show initiative.

Common SRT themes: accidents, natural disasters, conflict situations, moral dilemmas, resource shortages. In every case, the ideal response involves taking charge, helping others, and acting within ethical limits.

Don't overthink. Write your genuine instinctive response. Assessors distrust overly polished answers — they look for spontaneity and character.
SD — Self Description
5 perspectives~15 minutes

You write what five people think of you: your parents, teacher, friend, yourself, and the person you want to become. Assessors compare your self-description with how you actually behave in GTO tasks and your IO interview. Inconsistency is a red flag.

Be honest and self-aware. Mention genuine weaknesses alongside genuine strengths. Don't describe a perfect person — describe a real one who is growing.
DAY 3–4 — STAGE 2

GTO — Group Testing Officer Tasks

The GTO series is the most physically and socially demanding part of SSB. You are observed in group and individual settings across 9 tasks over two days.

Group Discussion (GD)

Two rounds: one on a current affairs topic, one on a concrete problem. No appointed leader. Assessors watch your content, listening ability, and group dynamics contribution.

Group Planning Exercise (GPE)

A model terrain is shown. A scenario is read out. You write your individual plan, then the group discusses and presents a unified plan. Tests planning, synthesis, and communication.

Progressive Group Task (PGT)

The group must cross an obstacle course using limited material (planks, ropes, poles). Four obstacles of increasing difficulty. No appointed leader — leadership emerges organically.

Half Group Task (HGT)

Same as PGT but with half the group (7–8 candidates). More individual pressure. Less room to hide.

Lecturette

Choose one of four topics. 3 minutes to prepare, 3 minutes to speak to your group. Assessed on fluency, structure, confidence, and content.

Command Task

You are made commander of 2–3 subordinates (other candidates). Complete an obstacle using given resources. Tests your leadership style — do you command, delegate, and take responsibility?

Final Group Task (FGT)

Full group. One obstacle. Treated as a final opportunity. High energy, decisive action, and collaborative spirit all matter.

Individual Obstacles (IO)

10 obstacles graded 1–10 by difficulty. Attempted alone, scored on the total points. Tests physical confidence and risk-taking within ability.

Snake Race / Group Obstacle Race

Physical team relay. Less formally assessed, but energy, sportsmanship, and camaraderie observed.

GTO Golden Rule: Do something. Inaction is the worst outcome. A wrong idea, stated confidently and amended when challenged, is far better than silence.
DAY 4–5

The Personal Interview

A 45–60 minute one-on-one with the Interviewing Officer (IO). This is not a quiz — it is a structured conversation designed to understand who you are. The IO has already read your PIQ (Personal Information Questionnaire) filled on Day 1.

Phase 1: Rapport Building

The IO creates a comfortable atmosphere. Casual questions about your journey, hometown, family. Be natural — this is where genuine personality shows.

Phase 2: Family & Background

Parents' professions, siblings, upbringing, values instilled at home. The IO is building a picture of your foundational influences.

Phase 3: Education & Career

Why you chose your stream, academic performance, college activities, failures and how you handled them.

Phase 4: Defence Motivation

Why the Armed Forces specifically? What do you know about the service you're applying for? Which regiment/branch appeals to you and why? Be specific and genuine.

Phase 5: Current Affairs & GK

Geo-political situations, defence news, national issues. Not a test of facts — a test of whether you have an informed, considered opinion.

Phase 6: Self-Assessment

Strengths, weaknesses, life goals, role models, challenges overcome. Consistency with your SD test is critical here.

Interview truth: The IO is not trying to trick you. They are trying to know you. The candidate who is most self-aware and most honest — not most polished — tends to fare best.
PREPARATION

How to Actually Prepare

📖

Read daily

The Hindu every morning. Focus on defence, foreign policy, and social issues. Build genuine opinions — the IO can spot borrowed ones immediately.

🪞

Self-awareness first

Write your PIQ answers now, before you go. Know your own story — your decisions, your values, your turning points. The IO asks about you.

🏃

Physical fitness

For Individual Obstacles and the overall stamina test. Run 5km daily. Do pull-ups and dips. Physical confidence translates directly into psychological confidence.

🗣️

Speak out loud

Practice Lecturette and GD out loud, not just in your head. Fluency under pressure requires repetition. Use the Practice module on this site.

✍️

Write TAT/WAT daily

30 seconds is brutally short. The only way to get consistently positive, well-structured stories is repetition. Ten TATs a day for a month makes a measurable difference.

🧘

Be, don't perform

The single most common mistake: trying to perform OLQs rather than genuinely having them. Develop the qualities in daily life. Let SSB observe what already exists.

"The best preparation for SSB is becoming a better human being."
— A common refrain among recommended candidates
